Apple's Advanced Data Protection may be back in the UK. Good — but there is a better way: Be ungovernable

Apple’s move was the lesser of two evils. The original request from the UK government was for the government to have a backdoor to end-to-end encryption. Anyone who knows even a tiny bit about this technology acknowledges that there is no such thing as “backdoor access, but only for the right people”. Furthermore, even if there were, no one is the right people. Such changes are basically security nightmares.
